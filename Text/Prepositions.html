Prepositions and particles

ἐπευφήμησαν

(Hom. Il. 1.22)

("They shouted assent")

ἐπευφήμησαν "they shouted assent, approved, applauded", verb 3rd pl aor ind act homeric ionic unaugmented, from ἐπευφημέω, ἐπί ("subsequently, after", or simply intensifying the verb) + εὐφημέω ("to cheer, shout in triumph, to applaud"). εὐφημέω in turn is formed with εὐ ("well") + φημί ("to speak").

ἐπί: "subsequently, after", or simply intensifying the verb.

κρατερὸν δ᾽ ἐπὶ μῦθον ἔτελλε

(Hom. Il. 1.25)

("and laid upon (him) a stern command")

ἐπὶ (ἐπί): "upon".

ἔτελλε: "to complete, accomplish", from τέλλω. However, it seems there is here a poetic split of the composite verb ἐπὶ + τέλλω, ἐπιτέλλω, which means "to lay upon, enjoin, prescribe, ordain, command, order".

μῦθον: "a word, speech", or anything delivered speaking (in opposition to ἔργον), so also "a counsel, advice, a command, order".

κρατερὸν: "harsh, rough".
