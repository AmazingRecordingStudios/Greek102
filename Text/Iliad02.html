Iliad 1 (vv.8-ff)

τίς τ᾽ ἄρ σφωε θεῶν ἔριδι ξυνέηκε μάχεσθαι;

(Hom. Il. 1.8)

("And which of the gods, then, brought these two into a dispute, fighting?")

τίς: "who? which?".

ἄρ (epic form of ἄρᾰ): "then".

σφωε: "them" (of two persons), dual.

θεῶν: "of the gods", DAT pl.

ξυνέηκε: "brought", verb 3rd sg aor ind act epic sig_to_ci, from συνίημι .

ἔριδι: "quarrel", noun sg fem DAT (NOM: ἔρις").

μάχεσθαι: "to fight", verb pres inf mp, from μάχομαι.

ὃ γὰρ βασιλῆϊ χολωθεὶς

(Hom. Il. 1.9)

("Because he was angered with the king")

γὰρ: "because, for".

ὃ "he".

χολωθεὶς: "was angered", part sg aor pass masc nom, from verb χολόω, derived from noun χόλος, "gall, bile, bitter anger".

βασιλῆϊ: "with the king", DAT sg (epic/ionic).

νοῦσον ἀνὰ στρατὸν ὄρσε κακήν

(Hom. Il. 1.10)

("a pestilence he sent upon the army, wicked")

νοῦσον: "sickness, disease, malady", fem sg ACC (NOM ionic: νοῦσος, NOM attic: νόσος).

κακήν "bad, wicked", adg fem sg ACC, from κάκη.

ὄρσε "arised, started up", verb 3rd sg aor ind act epic unaugmented, from ὄρνυμι.

κακήν "bad, wicked", adg fem sg ACC, from κάκη.

ἀνὰ στρατὸν "upon the army".
