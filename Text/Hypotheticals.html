Hypotheticals and other conditional clauses

μή σε γέρον κοίλῃσιν ἐγὼ παρὰ νηυσὶ κιχείω

(Hom. Il. 1.26)

("They shouted assent")

μή: "let not, do not", negative conjunction, used with aor. subjunctive to form the Prohibitive Subjunctive, cf https://ancientgreek.pressbooks.com/chapter/46/

κιχείω: "catch, meet with", aor subj 1st pers sg, from κιχάνω.

σε: "you", ACC sg (nom: σύ).

μή σε .. ἐγὼ .. κιχείω: "let me not catch you .."

ἀλλ᾽ ἴθι μή μ᾽ ἐρέθιζε σαώτερος ὥς κε νέηαι.

(Hom. Il. 1.32)

(" But go, do not anger me, that you may return the safer.”")

ἴθι: "go", verb 2nd sg pres imperat act, from εἶμι.

μή μ᾽ ἐρέθιζε: "do not anger me", verb 2nd sg pres imperat act, from ἐρεθίζω.
