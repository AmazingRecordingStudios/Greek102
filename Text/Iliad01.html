Iliad 1

ἣ μυρί᾽ Ἀχαιοῖς ἄλγε᾽ ἔθηκεν

(Hom. Il. 1.2)

("that countless pains caused to the Acheans")

ἣ: "that, which", relative pronoun, sg fem NOM, from ὅς, ὅ, ἣ.

μυρί᾽ ἄλγε᾽: "countless pains", noun pl neut ACC, elided form of μυρία ἄλγεα.

ἔθηκεν (or ἔθηκε, without movable nu): "caused", verb 3rd sg aor ind act, from τίθημι (that can mean: to set, put, place; to put in a certain state, to make, cause).

Ἀχαιοῖς: DAT pl.

αὐτοὺς δὲ ἑλώρια τεῦχε κύνεσσιν
οἰωνοῖσί τε δαῖτα

(Hom. Il. 1.4-5)

("and made them prey for dogs
and feast for vultures.")

αὐτούς (or αὐτοὺς with grave accent within a sentence): "them", pron pers 3rd pl. ACC, from αὐτός , αὐτό , αὐτή, that when used without article in oblique cases, stands as the 3rd person personal pronoun, "he, she, it, they".

τεῦχε : "he made", verb 3rd sg imperf ind act homeric ionic unaugmented, from τεύχω ("to make").

ἑλώρια : "prey", noun pl neut acc, from NOM ἑλώριον, which is a variant (possibly a poetic license) from ἕλωρ ("booty, spoil, prey"), which in turn derives from the verb αἱρέω , "to grasp, seize, take with the hand".

κύνεσσιν : "for dogs", DAT pl, from κύων ("dog").

δαῖτα : "feast, banquet", noun fem ACC sg, from δαίτη poetic form of δαίς, ("a feast, banquet").

οἰωνοῖσί : "for vultures", DAT pl, from οἰωνός ("a large bird, bird of prey", such as a vulture or eagle, and so distinguished from a common bird).
