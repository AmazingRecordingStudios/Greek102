Iliad 1

ἣ μυρί᾽ Ἀχαιοῖς ἄλγε᾽ ἔθηκεν

(Hom. Il. 1.2)

("that countless pains caused to the Acheans")

ἣ: "that, which", relative pronoun, sg fem NOM, from ὅς, ὅ, ἣ.

μυρί᾽ ἄλγε᾽: "countless pains", noun pl neut ACC, elided form of μυρία ἄλγεα.

ἔθηκεν (or ἔθηκε, without movable nu): "caused", verb 3rd sg aor ind act, from τίθημι (that can mean: "to set, put, place; to put in a certain state, to make, cause").

Ἀχαιοῖς: DAT pl.

αὐτοὺς δὲ ἑλώρια τεῦχε κύνεσσιν
οἰωνοῖσί τε δαῖτα

(Hom. Il. 1.4-5)

("and made them prey for dogs
and feast for crows.")

αὐτούς (or αὐτοὺς with grave accent within a sentence): "them", pron pers 3rd pl. ACC, from αὐτός , αὐτό , αὐτή, that when used without article in oblique cases, stands as the 3rd person personal pronoun, "he, she, it, they".

τεῦχε : "he made", verb 3rd sg imperf ind act homeric ionic unaugmented, from τεύχω ("to make").

ἑλώρια : "prey", noun pl neut acc, from NOM ἑλώριον, which is a variant (possibly a poetic license) from ἕλωρ ("booty, spoil, prey"), which in turn derives from the verb αἱρέω , "to grasp, seize, take with the hand".

κύνεσσιν : "for dogs", DAT pl, from κύων ("dog").

δαῖτα : "feast, banquet", noun fem ACC sg, from δαίτη poetic form of δαίς, ("a feast, banquet").

οἰωνοῖσί : "for vultures", DAT pl, from οἰωνός ("a large bird, bird of prey", such as a vulture or eagle, and so distinguished from a common bird).

Ἄϊδι προΐαψεν

(Hom. Il. 1.3)

("it sent forth into Ades")

προΐαψεν : "it sent forth", verb 3rd sg aor ind act nu_movable raw_preverb, from προϊάπτω (προ, "forth" + ἰάπτω, "to send forward").

Ἄϊδι : noun sg masc DAT poetic. NOM: Ἅιδης. A possible ethymology might be ἀ- (privative prefix) + ἰδεῖν (to see), therefore "the place where there is no sight".

Διὸς δ᾽ ἐτελείετο βουλή

(Hom. Il. 1.5)

("and Zeus' will was being fulfilled.")

βουλή : "will, determination, plan, design", NOM sg fem. The corresponding verb is βούλομαι ("to will, wish, be willing").

Διός : "of Zeus", GEN sg (NOM: Ζεύς ).

ἐτελείετο : "will, determination, plan, design", verb 3rd sg imperf ind mp, from τελέω ("to fulfil, accomplish, execute, perform").

ἐξ οὗ δὴ τὰ πρῶτα διαστήτην ἐρίσαντε
Ἀτρεΐδης τε ἄναξ ἀνδρῶν καὶ δῖος Ἀχιλλεύς.

(Hom. Il. 1.6-7)

("[sing] from the time when, for the first time, went at odds, quarreling,
the son of Atreus and lord of men, and the noble Achilles.")

δή (or δὴ with grave accent within a phrase): "precisely", partic indeclform.

ἐξ οὗ: "from the time when". ἐξ ("from") + GEN. οὗ is the neut. GEN of the relative pronoun ὅς, ὅ, ἣ.

τὰ πρῶτα : "for the first time".

διαστήτην : "they went at odds", verb 3rd dual aor ind act homeric ionic unaugmented, from διίστημι (from διά + ἵστημι , "stay separated").

ἐρίσαντε : "quarreling", part dual aor act masc nom, from ἐρίζω.

ἄναξ : "noun sg masc nom", NOM sg.
